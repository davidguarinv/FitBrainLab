Our Focus

Exercise & Stress Resilience

Our lab investigates how physical activity can enhance stress resilience in university students, developing evidence-based interventions to improve mental wellbeing.

  • Neurobiological Mechanisms

    Investigating how exercise affects brain function and stress response pathways.

  • Exercise Interventions

    Developing and testing targeted physical activity programs for student populations.

  • Habit Formation

    Studying how sustainable exercise habits develop and persist in academic environments.

Try This Today

Change Starts Small

You don't need exhausting 3 hour work out sessions to reap the benefits of exercise. Here are some simple habits you can start applying right now.

5-Minute Study Break

For every hour of studying, take a 5-minute break to do jumping jacks, stretches, or a quick walk. Our research shows this improves information retention by 18%.

Morning Movement

Start your day with 10 minutes of gentle movement. Try stretching, jumping jacks, or a brisk walk. This activates your brain and improves focus for your first classes.

Social Movement

Invite a friend to join you for a 20-minute walk or workout. Social exercise increases enjoyment by 26% and makes you more likely to stick with it long-term.
