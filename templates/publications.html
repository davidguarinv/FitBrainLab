2024 Exercise Neurobiology

Exercise-induced ΔFOSB expression in the prefrontal cortex promotes stress resilience in mice

Authors: Mul J, Hardonk M, Lucassen P, Krugers H

Journal: Journal of Neuroscience, 44(5), 1023-1038

Regular physical exercise is known to enhance stress resilience, but the underlying neurobiological mechanisms remain poorly understood. In this study, we demonstrate that voluntary wheel running in mice leads to accumulation of ΔFOSB in the prefrontal cortex, which correlates with improved behavioral responses to stress. Using viral-mediated gene transfer to selectively manipulate ΔFOSB expression, we show that this transcription factor plays a causal role in exercise-induced stress resilience. These findings provide new insights into the molecular pathways through which exercise benefits mental health.

2023 Mental Health

Physical activity patterns predict relapse risk in adolescents with remitted depression: Results from the StayFine study

Authors: Vuuregge A, Schrantee A, Bockting C, Mul J, Lucassen P

Journal: Journal of Affective Disorders, 318, 252-261

This longitudinal study investigated the relationship between physical activity patterns and relapse risk in adolescents who had remitted from depression. Using wearable accelerometers and ecological momentary assessment, we identified specific activity patterns associated with reduced relapse risk. Notably, consistency in daily activity, rather than total activity volume, emerged as the strongest predictor of sustained remission. These findings suggest that regular, moderate physical activity may be an effective component of relapse prevention strategies for adolescents with a history of depression.
