Research Project

LEOPARD PREDICT

Predicting antidepressant treatment response using neuroimaging and physical activity data

Project Overview

It usually takes around eight weeks to determine if an antidepressant drug is effective in depressed patients. Furthermore, after one year, treatment with antidepressants is effective in about half of all treated depressed patients. In the LEOPARD PREDICT study, we will determine if we can predict antidepressant-treatment response in depressed patients (18-60 years) within two weeks of starting with antidepressant treatment using resting-state functional MRI (rsfMRI) data.

We will explore if integration of physical activity parameters can improve prediction of antidepressant treatment effectivity using rsfMRI data. This approach could significantly reduce the time needed to identify effective treatments for individual patients, leading to faster symptom relief and improved outcomes.

Research Methodology

Our research methodology combines advanced neuroimaging with physical activity monitoring:

  • Resting-state functional MRI (rsfMRI) to assess brain connectivity patterns before and after treatment initiation
  • Wearable accelerometers to continuously monitor physical activity levels and patterns
  • Standardized clinical assessments to measure depression severity and treatment response

Current Progress

The LEOPARD PREDICT study is currently in its implementation phase. We have successfully:

  • Developed the neuroimaging protocols and analysis pipelines for rsfMRI data
  • Established the physical activity monitoring methodology using validated wearable devices
  • Initiated participant recruitment across multiple clinical sites

We are currently collecting baseline data from the first cohort of participants and developing the machine learning algorithms that will be used to predict treatment response.

Expected Outcomes

Through this research, we aim to:

  • Develop a predictive model that can identify likely responders to antidepressant treatment within two weeks of initiation
  • Determine how physical activity patterns interact with brain function to influence treatment response
  • Create clinical guidelines for incorporating physical activity assessments into depression treatment planning

Significance

If successful, this research could transform the treatment approach for depression by:

  • Reducing the trial-and-error period in antidepressant treatment from 8+ weeks to just 2 weeks
  • Providing objective biomarkers for treatment response that complement clinical assessments
  • Highlighting the role of physical activity in depression treatment and recovery
