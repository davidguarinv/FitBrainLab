Human Research Project

STRIDE-4-Mental Health

Promoting exercise persistence and mental wellbeing in university students

Project Overview

Many young adults face significant lifestyle changes and experience stress when starting a university education, increasing their risk for mental health issues like anxiety and depression. Regular exercise is a simple, low-cost way to boost mental well-being, reduce anxiety and depression, and even enhance academic performance.

The STRIDE-4-Mental Health project will apply a comprehensive network approach to uncover the factors influencing exercise persistence in students and its relationships to mental health, personality, and lifestyle (e.g., urbanicity). In collaboration with Caring Universities and the University Sports Centre, we will conduct large-scale assessment on physical activity patterns, factors influencing lasting exercise routines and their impact on (first year) student's stress, mental health and academic performance.

Insights will shape an online behaviour-change intervention combining psychoeducation, strategic planning, and a buddy system, which will be evaluated through a randomized controlled trial.

Research Methodology

Our research methodology employs a multi-faceted approach:

  • Network analysis to map the complex relationships between exercise habits, mental health, and academic performance
  • Large-scale surveys and ecological momentary assessments to capture real-time data on student behaviors and experiences
  • Randomized controlled trial to evaluate the effectiveness of our behavior-change intervention

Current Progress

The STRIDE-4-Mental Health project is currently in its initial phase. We have successfully:

  • Established partnerships with university sports centers and student health services
  • Developed comprehensive assessment protocols for measuring exercise habits, mental health, and academic performance
  • Designed the framework for our online behavior-change intervention

We are currently recruiting first-year university students for the initial assessment phase and finalizing the development of our intervention platform.

Expected Outcomes

Through this research, we aim to:

  • Identify the key factors that influence exercise persistence in university students
  • Map the complex relationships between exercise habits, mental health, and academic performance
  • Develop and validate an effective online intervention to promote exercise persistence and mental wellbeing
  • Create implementation guidelines for universities to support student mental health through exercise promotion

Practical Applications

The findings from this project will have direct applications for:

  • University student health services seeking to implement evidence-based mental health promotion strategies
  • University sports centers aiming to increase student engagement and retention
  • Student support services developing orientation programs for first-year students
